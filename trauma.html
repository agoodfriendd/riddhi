What I Did to Your Trauma

You told me things. And the fact that you trusted me with those parts of yourself, the painful parts, the parts you've been quietly trying to heal, that meant something. That should have meant everything to how I treated you.

Instead, I used that closeness carelessly. I moved in ways that I knew, or should have known, would hit exactly where you were already bruised. And there's no way to frame that that doesn't make it sound like what it was: a betrayal.

You'd already been hurt by people who were supposed to show up for you. You were already working through that. And then I came along, someone you saw as safe, as family, and I added to it. I became another entry in a list that should have stopped growing a long time ago.

That's the thing I can't shake. You trusted me specifically because you thought I was different. Because of what we were to each other. And I used that trust in the worst possible way, not by being a stranger who didn't know better, but by being someone who absolutely did.

Your pain is not dramatic. It's not you overreacting. What you felt was a completely real response to something that genuinely happened. And you had every right to feel every bit of it.
