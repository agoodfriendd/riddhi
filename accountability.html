This Is On Me

I'm not going to tell you it was a misunderstanding. I'm not going to say I didn't realize what I was doing, or that I was going through something, or that I didn't mean for it to go that far. None of that changes what actually happened to you.

I did this. Me. Not circumstances, not bad timing, not some complicated situation that spiralled. I made decisions — multiple ones — that led to you being hurt. And I have to sit with that.

The hardest part of actually taking accountability — not just saying the words — is accepting that I don't get to decide how you receive this. I don't get to tell you how to feel about it, or when to get over it, or what the "right" response to my apology is. That's yours. Completely.

What I can do is be honest: I was wrong. Not "wrong but—" Just wrong. And I'd rather you be furious with me and know the truth than have you think for even one second that any of this was your fault.

It wasn't. You didn't do anything wrong. You trusted someone who should have been trustworthy, and he wasn't. That's the only story here.
