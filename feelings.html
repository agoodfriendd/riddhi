Playing with Your Feelings

This is the part I'm most ashamed of. Because this wasn't something that just happened to you. This was something I did to you.

You felt things. Real things. And somewhere along the way I decided, consciously or not, that those feelings were something I could just play around with. Like they were flexible. Like they'd just bounce back. Like you'd just bounce back.

But you're a person. With actual feelings that actually hurt when someone messes with them. And the fact that I needed to remind myself of that, the fact that I forgot it long enough to do what I did, says a lot about how I failed you.

What makes it worse is that I knew you genuinely. I wasn't some stranger who misread signals. I knew your heart. I knew how you feel things deeply. I knew that you don't just switch emotions on and off. And I still did it.

You never should have had to wonder whether what you were feeling was being taken seriously. You never should have been left confused, second-guessing yourself, or feeling stupid for caring. That's on me. All of it.
