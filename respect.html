What You Actually Deserve

You've always been someone who gives a lot — to the people around you, to the people you care about. You show up. You feel things fully. You're loyal in a way that not many people actually are. And because of that, you've also been the person who ends up getting hurt the most when someone doesn't match that.

I was supposed to be different. Not even as a partner — just as someone in your life. As a best friend. As the person who was basically your brother. That's the role I had, and that comes with a responsibility that I completely failed to honour.

You deserved someone who looked at your trust and protected it. Not someone who looked at it and thought about what they could get away with. I was the second kind. And that is a failure of character, not just a mistake.

I'm not going to tell you what to do with this — how much space to give, whether to forgive, whether to stay or go. That's not mine to decide. What I will say is: whatever you choose, choose what actually feels right to you. Not what's polite, not what's easy, not what gets rid of the discomfort fastest. What's actually right for you.

You've spent enough time adjusting yourself around other people's feelings. You don't have to do that here too.

I see you, Riddhi. I always have. I just didn't act like it when it mattered most — and I'm sorry for that more than anything else.

Whatever you decide, I'll respect it.
Take care of yourself first. Always.
