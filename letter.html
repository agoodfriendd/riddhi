Dear Riddhi,

I don't know how to start this. I've been staring at this for a while now because nothing I write feels right, not because I don't know what I want to say, but because I know nothing I say is going to be enough. And I need to say it anyway.

You are one of the most important people in my life. You're not just a friend; you're someone I look at like an idol. Someone I was supposed to protect, not hurt. And I hurt you.

I played with your feelings. I knew exactly what I was doing and I did it anyway. That's the part that's been eating me alive. Not that I made a mistake. But that I made a choice.

And on top of that, I triggered things in you that were already painful. Things you'd been carrying for a long time. Things that were never supposed to be touched like that, especially not by me. Of all people, NOT BY ME.

I keep thinking about what it must have felt like, to have the person you trusted like a brother be the one who opened up old traumas. That thought genuinely makes me sick. Because that's not who I want to be. That's not who I thought I was.

But I was. And I have to own that completely.

This isn't me asking for forgiveness right now, because I cannot even forgive myself. This is me just needing you to know: I see it. I see what I did, I see how wrong it was, and I'm not going to sit here and make excuses or act like it was complicated. It wasn't. I was wrong.
